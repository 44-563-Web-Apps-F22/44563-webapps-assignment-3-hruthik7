About Vampire Bat

Vampire bats, species of the subfamily Desmodontinae, are leaf-nosed bats found in Central and South America. Their food source is blood, a dietary trait called hematophagy. Three extant bat species feed solely on blood: the common vampire bat, the hairy-legged vampire bat, and the white-winged vampire bat.

Facts about Vampire Bats

  1. Vampire bats have fewer teeth than any other bat because they do not have to chew their food.
  2. Along with flying, vampire bats can run, jump, and hop with great speed, using their chest muscles to fling themselves skyward.
  3. Scientists recently discovered that the anticoagulant in vampire bat saliva is twenty times stronger than any other known anti-clotting agent.
  4. With more research we may find this substance can help with serious human disorders like heart attacks and strokes.
